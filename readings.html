Books

Reading notes

Short reflections, not formal reviews.

Oct 2018 - Feb 2020

Steve Jobs

Walter Isaacson

A detailed biography of Steve Jobs, from childhood through the end of his life.

The book made me think about conviction, compromise, and the cost of stubbornness. I admired Jobs for staying with ideas he believed in even under pressure, while also seeing how the same trait created failures.

The final chapters were emotional and made me value health, time, and reflection more.

March 2019 - April 2019

Outliers: The Story of Success

Malcolm Gladwell

Gladwell argues that extreme success is shaped not only by effort, but also by environment, culture, timing, and opportunity.

I felt the book defined success narrowly, but I agreed with the broader point that environment can deeply shape behavior and outcomes.

The Korean Air example stood out because it connects culture, communication, hierarchy, and safety.

October 2019

Talking To Strangers

Malcolm Gladwell

The book explores why people misunderstand strangers and why communication can fail.

I did not enjoy this one as much. The central idea that people often default to truth felt intuitive, and many examples made the argument harder rather than clearer for me.

November 2019

Mindset

Carol S. Dweck

The book explains fixed and growth mindsets, and how both can show up in everyday life.

The examples helped me notice fixed-mindset moments in myself, especially around criticism and fear of mistakes.

December 2019 - Jan 2020

Open Heart: A Cardiac Surgeon's Stories of Life and Death on the Operating Table

Stephen Westaby

A memoir about cardiac surgery, heart transplants, and artificial hearts.

The medical vocabulary was challenging, but the stories were eye-opening. One idea stayed with me: surgery can be a daily routine for the surgeon and a once-in-a-lifetime crisis for the patient.

That contrast made me think about empathy, trust, and communication in high-stakes relationships.

December 2019 - Now
